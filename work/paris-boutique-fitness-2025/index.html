What is this report?

Paris has been an underdog in boutique fitness among European capitals. But over the past few years, new studios have popped up and chains have grown rapidly. This report provides an overview of the Parisian boutique fitness market, the evolution of different modalities, and their geographic distribution. It is based on a large representative data set. Exact studio counts are not fully accurate but relative percentages and growth numbers capture real insights.

Paris is still early in its fitness days, but the industry is growing fast. Until 2010, there were only about 1-5 openings a year in our data set. But then yearly openings start rising quickly, with 75 studios opening in 2024. While these numbers are only representative, the growth is clearly visible and it will be interesting to see over the next years whether the trajectory holds or if the market starts to become saturated.

What are people training for?

The Modality Landscape

Distribution of boutique studios by primary modalities. Pilates and yoga dominate, with reformer pilates showing explosive growth.

Studios by Modality

Top 15 modalities · 644 studios total

Studios can appear in multiple modalities; counts are locations, not unique brands. Categories have been consolidated (e.g., all yoga variants → "yoga").

Pilates and yoga dominate the industry with almost half of all studios (49%) offering pilates classes. Most of these studios also offer yoga (46% of all studios have yoga classes). After that, strength training and hiit/bootcamp studios have strong presences (34% and 29%). Traditional female modalities still dominate the landscape and drive the most trends in the industry, while more male-leaning modalities seem to be reminiscent of traditional fitness.

Multi-modality studios are everywhere. Only 30.3% of studios offer just one modality, while the majority (69.7%) offer multiple. But with more and more unique and hybrid concepts, even the idea of a single modality needs to be questioned. Rather, we are starting to see variations on multi-modality: classes fusing different exercises into one, flagship locations with open gym space, and of course studios offering a primary modality but filling extra space/time with complementary classes.

The Reformer Pilates Boom

Rising Star

Reformer pilates has exploded in Paris, with 50.6% of studios opening in 2024 and 2025 alone. The trend shows no signs of slowing.

Reformer Pilates Studios by Opening Year

115 studios total

Includes lagree studios (reformer-based method). Opening dates are estimated from location pages and domain registration data. Dates are adjusted to account for domain registration typically preceding actual studio openings by ~2 months.

The reformer pilates trend is remarkable with hip neighborhoods seeing new studios on nearly every street. It's hard to see how this will last, especially with class drop-in prices around 40€-55€. Many in the industry are skeptical of this boom – most instructors have limited training, many with no formal pilates background, studio owners face high startup costs and Paris rents, and differentiation is low.

Of the 62 new pilates studios, 43 offer reformer classes. This is even more stark when you look at the growth of pilates as a whole. 20.7% of all pilates studios opened in the past 16 months*, although 37.4% of reformer studios opened in that period, again highlighting its dominance. Low-impact (which overlaps with a lot of pilates classes studios) has seen the second-largest growth, with 27.9% of these studios opening since mid-2024. This also shows the shift toward less-stress movement, likely caused by greater awareness of longevity and a maturing of the client population.

*Note that this is probably more accurately the past 12-14 months since opening dates are estimated based on domain registration.

Single Studios vs. Chains

The Boutique Model

Distribution of studio brands by number of locations. The vast majority operate as single-location studios, reflecting the personalized, neighborhood-focused nature of boutique fitness as well as the challenges studios face in becoming profitable and expanding.

Studio Brands by Number of Locations

Studios are grouped by domain (or name if no domain). Most boutique fitness brands remain single-location operations.

One indication of the relative newness of boutique fitness is the extreme prominence of single-location businesses. It's likely that we will start seeing greater consolidation as "winners" develop truly profitable business models and smaller studios are purchased or forced to close.
