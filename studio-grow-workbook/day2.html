STEP one
1

Discovery

Places (IG, TikTok, Google, local)

Stages (Gen Z, 45-yo mom)

1. Signage: Windows, door, banners, yard signs ("a yogi lives here"), flags, and air dancers

2. Digital channels: Google, Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, YouTube, TikTok, LinkedIn, plus internal and external signage, referrals, email/SMS, local events, corporate partnerships, and direct mail

STEP two
2

Investigation

Places (Google reviews, Social, website)

Stage

Google Reviews: 250+ (Once you reach this milestone, you can typically reduce your marketing budget by half)

1. Back of front door: "Hey, if it's been a great session, would you mind scanning that QR code and leaving us a review? Tell us what you loved the most!"

2. Include review request in your intro pack

STEP three
3

Lead Phase

Places (website, contact, DM, account)

Capture: Name, email, phone

Follow-up sequence:

1. Initial invitation: "I would love to invite you in / have you book a class..."

2. (5 minutes later) SMS/WhatsApp with the same information

3. (1 day later) Offer a very specific time slot (via email or SMS)

4. Send FAQs about the intro package

5. Share success stories

6. Send two additional follow-up messages

STEP four
4

Intro Package

Places (social, GMB, website, ads)

1. Web/landing page - set clear expectations

2. Thank you page/email/SMS/WhatsApp confirmation

Website/landing page must answer:

1. How much does it cost?

2. How regularly should I attend?

3. What should I expect from my first session?

4. When will you be chatting with me? (This is non-negotiable - it's part of our process)

STEP five
5

Pre Session

Places (in studio)

1. Confirm their first session (ideally from the teacher or the front desk person that day)

2. Gain confidence, reduce anxiety

3. Signage

4. Leave a note or share with instructors who is coming in for first time

6

During Session

Recognizable:

1. Colored mat

2. Crystal or rock at their place

3. Set up for them

Name: Pull a photo from IG/FB

7

a. Feeling great: beeline, check-in: how it went, do they have next session booked and if any questions

b. Front desk: follow-through; Google Review

3. Text: asks how they're doing

4. FAQ/humanistic emails
